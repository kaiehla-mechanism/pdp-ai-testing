Engineering explainer

Why three stages change everything.

01
Three-stage telescoping columns Standard standing desks use two-stage legs — more gap between sections, more lateral play at height. Vernal's three-stage system distributes the extension across three sections, each with engineered glide tolerances under 0.001″. The result: near-zero sway at any height.
02
T-frame, not C-frame C-frames anchor legs at the back of the desktop, leaving the front half unsupported. Vernal's T-frame crossbar runs center-span, distributing load front-to-back and side-to-side simultaneously — stable in all four planes at all heights.
03
Independent motor per column Dual motors — one per leg — synchronize to <0.8mm height variance across the span. If one leg meets resistance, both stop and reverse. No torque imbalance. No tilting under uneven loads.
04
Third-party test report — publicly available Every specification is verified by accredited independent labs, not self-reported. Load capacity, noise level, lift cycles, column gap tolerance. We publish the full report. Most brands do not offer this.
